Seco de Pollo recipe.

Seco de Pollo is a popular Ecuadorian dish that translates to "chicken stew." It's a flavorful and aromatic dish that showcases the rich culinary heritage of Ecuador. The dish is known for its tender, slow-cooked chicken pieces simmered in a fragrant sauce made from a blend of ingredients such as onions, peppers, garlic, and various spices. The name "seco" refers to the reduction of the sauce during cooking, resulting in a flavorful and concentrated gravy-like consistency.

Ingredients:

    2-3 pounds chicken pieces (legs, thighs, or a combination)
    1 large red onion, finely chopped
    2-3 cloves garlic, minced
    1 red bell pepper, diced
    1 teaspoon ground cumin
    1 teaspoon ground achiote (annatto) or paprika
    1 teaspoon ground coriander
    1 teaspoon dried oregano
    2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    2 cups chicken broth
    1 cup beer or water
    1 cup chopped cilantro leaves
    Salt and pepper, to taste
    Optional: diced potatoes for added texture

Steps:

    Season and Sear the Chicken: Season the chicken pieces with salt, pepper, ground cumin, ground achiote, and ground coriander. Let them marinate for about 15-30 minutes.
    Saute Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and diced red bell pepper. Saut√© until the onion is translucent and the pepper is softened.
    Simmer the Chicken: Return the seared chicken pieces to the pot. Add the dried oregano and chopped cilantro. Pour in the chicken broth and beer (or water).
    Adjust Consistency: If you'd like a thicker sauce, you can remove the chicken from the pot and continue to simmer the sauce uncovered until it thickens to your desired consistency.
    Serve: Serve Seco de Pollo over cooked white rice, and accompany it with cooked beans or lentils. The dish is often garnished with additional chopped cilantro.
