Llapingacho recipe.

Llapingacho is a beloved Ecuadorian dish that represents comfort food at its finest. This hearty and flavorful dish consists of potato patties that are typically stuffed with cheese and served with a variety of accompaniments. Llapingacho showcases the native ingredient of potatoes, which hold a significant place in Ecuador's culinary traditions due to the country's diverse potato varieties.

Ingredients:

Potato Patties:

    4 large russet potatoes, peeled and boiled until tender
    1 cup shredded cheese (queso fresco or mozzarella)
    1 small onion, finely chopped
    1 teaspoon ground cumin
    Salt and pepper, to taste

Peanut Sauce:

    1 cup roasted peanuts, unsalted
    1 small onion, chopped
    2 cloves garlic, minced
    1/2 cup milk
    2 tablespoons oil
    Salt and pepper, to taste

For Serving:

    Sausages
    Fried eggs
    Sliced avocado
    Lettuce or salad

Steps:

    Prepare the Potato Patties: Mash the boiled potatoes until smooth and lump-free. Allow them to cool slightly.
    Shape and Fry the Patties: Take a portion of the potato mixture and flatten it in your hand. Place a small cube of cheese in the center and encase it with the potato mixture, forming a round patty.
    Prepare the Peanut Sauce: In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted peanuts, chopped onion, minced garlic, milk, oil, salt, and pepper. Blend until the mixture forms a smooth sauce. If needed, add a little water to achieve the desired consistency.
    Serve: Serve the llapingacho with the peanut sauce drizzled over the top. You can also serve it with sausages, fried eggs, sliced avocado, and a side salad for a complete meal.
