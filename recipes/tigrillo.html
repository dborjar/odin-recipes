Tigrillo recipe.

Tigrillo is a traditional Ecuadorian dish made from green plantains that are mashed and mixed with cheese and other ingredients. This flavorful and comforting dish is often enjoyed as a breakfast or brunch option, and it's popular in coastal regions of Ecuador. Tigrillo's name comes from the Spanish word "tigre," which means tiger, referring to the dish's speckled appearance from the bits of cheese mixed into the mashed plantains.

Ingredients:

    3-4 green plantains, peeled and chopped into chunks
    1 cup grated cheese (queso fresco or a similar white cheese)
    1 small onion, finely chopped
    2 tablespoons butter or oil
    1/2 cup milk
    Salt and pepper, to taste
    Optional: cooked scrambled eggs, cooked chorizo or sausage, avocado slices, aji hot sauce for serving

Steps:

    Prepare the Plantains: Peel the green plantains and chop them into chunks. Place the plantain chunks in a pot of salted water and boil them until they are tender when pierced with a fork, about 15-20 minutes. Drain the cooked plantains.
    Mash the Plantains: In a large bowl, mash the cooked plantains using a potato masher or fork. Add the butter or oil and mix it into the mashed plantains.
    Add Cheese and Onion: Stir in the grated cheese and finely chopped onion into the mashed plantains. The cheese will start to melt from the residual heat of the plantains.
    Add Milk and Seasoning: Gradually add the milk to the mixture while stirring, until you achieve a creamy and cohesive texture. If needed, you can adjust the amount of milk to your preferred consistency. - Season the tigrillo with salt and pepper to taste.
    Optional Additions: If desired, you can incorporate cooked scrambled eggs or cooked chorizo into the tigrillo for added protein and flavor.
    Serve: Serve the tigrillo warm. It's often accompanied by slices of avocado and aji hot sauce on the side. -Tigrillo can be enjoyed as a breakfast dish, a light meal, or a side dish alongside other Ecuadorian specialties.
